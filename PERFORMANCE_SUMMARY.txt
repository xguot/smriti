  Smriti_Robust     2.35         3.04          +0.69  ← barely moves
  Smriti_Default    2.28         3.15          +0.87

  MICE              1.61         4.38          +2.76
  FIML              1.32         4.77          +3.45  ← worst degradation

Smriti_Robust is the only method whose covariance recovery is
structurally unaffected by 5% outlier contamination (Δ +0.69 vs.
+2.4–3.5 for all competitors).  This is because the target manifold
is built from rank-based Spearman correlations and MAD scale estimates
before the Lagrangian routing step.  FIML — normally the gold standard
— degrades the most.

── λ TUNING ──

Tuning grid: λ ∈ {0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 1.0, 5.0} × robust ∈ {T, F}
on Normal and Lognormal (N ∈ {200, 500, 5000}, MAR, 100 reps).

  Normal, Pearson:  best λ = 0.01 (Frob = 1.74)
                     λ = 1.0 gives Frob = 2.05  (Δ +0.31)
  Normal, Robust:   best λ = 0.01 (Frob = 1.71)
                     λ = 1.0 gives Frob = 2.04  (Δ +0.33)

λ = 1.0 was selected as a universal default.  It is within 0.3
Frobenius units of the per-condition optimum on Normal data while
providing stronger constraint enforcement that benefits the Outlier
case (not included in the tuning grid but verified in production).
Users can reduce λ to ~0.01 for clean Normal data.

── KNOWN LIMITATIONS ──

1. LOGNORMAL FAILURE: Spearman/MAD targets collapse under skew
   (Frobenius 9.87, bias -88.9%).  Use smriti_fiml() or custom_target.

2. NORMAL MAR TRADE-OFF: Smriti has slightly higher Frobenius distance
   than missForest on clean Normal data (2.35 vs. 1.69).  It recovers
   slope variance better (-3.67% vs. -4.59%) but at the cost of
   slightly worse overall covariance fit.

3. INITIALISATION DEPENDENCY: Requires a pre-existing initial
   imputation.  Performance with column-mean fallback is lower.

4. APPROXIMATE MI: smriti_mi() uses bootstrap resampling, not
   full Bayesian posterior draws.  Standard errors may be slightly
   anti-conservative.
