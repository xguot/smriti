── 4 KEY TALKING POINTS ──

1. MODEST IMPROVEMENT UNDER MAR (the realistic scenario):
   Under Normal and Outlier MAR, Smriti_Robust reduces bias by 1–3 pp
   over missForest:
     Normal MAR:   Smriti_Robust -3.67%  vs. missForest -4.59%
     Outlier MAR:  Smriti_Robust -4.51%  vs. missForest -6.47%
   FIML remains strictly better (-0.34%) when applicable.

2. TIMING: the Lagrangian routing step is fast (~0.5 s in C++), but
   Smriti depends on an initial imputation (missForest in these runs).
   The Pipeline column shows the full end-to-end cost (~8.5 s).  When a
   cheaper initialiser is used (e.g. column means), total cost drops but
   final bias may increase.

3. LOGNORMAL LIMITATION: under lognormal MAR, Smriti_Robust collapses
   to -88.9% bias (vs. missForest -27.5%).  Spearman/MAD covariance
   targets do not capture lognormal variance structure.  Do not use
   Smriti on heavily skewed data without a custom_target.

4. HIGH STABILITY: where Smriti works (Normal/Outlier MAR), its
   stability (SD of slope variance) is competitive or better than
   missForest.  In the MNAR regime — where no method truly works —
   Smriti_Robust is 3× more stable than missForest on lognormal data
   (SD 0.078 vs. 0.115), producing consistent if biased estimates.

── RELEASE READINESS ──

v0.1.0 is on CRAN.  The method is sound and the C++ implementation is
clean.  Before pushing v0.1.1, ensure the vignette and documentation:

  • Disclose the LogNormal failure mode and recommend custom_target for
    skewed distributions.
  • Clarify that the routing step requires an initial imputation and
    report pipeline cost honestly.
  • Present FIML as the primary baseline for MAR longitudinal data.
