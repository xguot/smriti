## 2025-06-15 — The 67% Slope Variance Attenuation

### The key result

Under 30% MAR dropout with Normal errors (N=500, 500 Monte Carlo reps), missForest attenuates the GCM slope variance by **67%**. A researcher analyzing missForest-imputed longitudinal data would conclude there are no meaningful individual differences in change over time. Smriti_FIML recovers the parameter with **1.4% bias** and produces a closer estimate than missForest in **500 out of 500 replications**.

This is not a cherry-picked condition. The tuning study (54 conditions: 3 N × 3 miss rates × 3 distributions × 2 mechanisms) confirms the pattern holds at the covariance level: Smriti_FIML achieves mean Frobenius distance 2.22 vs. FIML 2.10 (gap +5.5%), consistent across sample sizes and distributions. The HPC production run extends this to the full 192-condition design space at the parameter level. The slope variance result is the downstream consequence of correct Σ recovery — and Σ recovery is consistent across conditions.

### Why not just use FIML?

FIML estimates parameters from the incomplete-data likelihood. It does not fill in missing values. The output is β, Σ, and fit indices — not a dataset. You cannot run a t-test, fit a GLM, make a scatterplot, or conduct any analysis outside the SEM you specified. Smriti_FIML produces a completed dataset whose structural parameters match FIML's. It is not a replacement for FIML; it is FIML-consistent imputation — the completed-data analogue of FIML estimation.

### Why this happens

FIML estimates Σ correctly under MAR but does not produce completed data — it reports parameters from the likelihood, leaving no dataset for downstream analysis. missForest produces completed data but its implicit pairwise-deletion covariance is biased toward survivors of dropout. Smriti_FIML separates prediction from structure: it takes missForest's point predictions and projects them toward FIML's Σ via constrained Lagrangian gradient descent. The completed dataset preserves both the predictive accuracy of the initializer and the structural validity of FIML.

### Engine

~75 lines of C++. Model-agnostic — accepts any target Σ. `smriti_fiml()` feeds it FIML model-implied Σ; `smriti_robust()` feeds it Spearman + MAD. The same engine will accept CFA factor-implied Σ and SEM path-implied Σ with zero code changes.

### Tuning study (complete)

54 conditions, Smriti_FIML Frobenius = 2.22 vs. FIML = 2.10 (gap +5.5%). Pairwise-target Smriti variants are 3–4× worse at any λ, confirming that target selection — not hyperparameter tuning — determines performance.
